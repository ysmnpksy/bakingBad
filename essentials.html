Our Baking Essentials

Rubber Spatula

This tool is idea for scraping the last bit of batter or dough out of a pan or for scraping all of the nooks and crannies out of a jar. Our favourite is Scion mini spatula, perfect for getting into the smallest of jars, check it out here.

Rolling Pin

Rolling pins are the best for rolling out cookie dough and pastry, but they also have a ton of other uses. Making blue meth rock candy for example? Put the shards in a zip lock bag and use the rolling pin to crush them up. Our favourite is by John Lewis, check it out here.

Fine-Mesh Sieve

You can put a sieve to work in a lot of different ways. For bakers, itâ€™s probably the most useful for sifting dry ingredients or adding a dusting of powdered sugar to a finished pan of brownies or cookies. Our favourite is by John Lewis. Check it out here.

Chef's Knife

If you only have one knife in your kitchen, this is the one you need. This knife is good for everything; slicing, dicing, chopping and mincing just about any ingredient. Our favourite is the SABATIER Stainless Steel Chef's Knife, check it out here.

Stand Mixer

These make mixing up doughs and batters much easier then you can imagine. And if that isn't enough, they make mixing much fater too. Our favourite is the Kenwood Chef Elite Stand Mixer, check it out here.

Silicone Mats

Parchment paper is great, but why not bake and cook with something reusable? Not only are silicone mats reusable, but they actually lay flat on cookie sheets! Using silicone mats allow cookies to be evenly bakes and slide right off the mat. Our favourite is by Silpat, check them out here.

Measuring Cups

Having a full set of measuring cups is a must; keep these tools in a easy-to-reach spot, because you'll use them all the time. Our favourite is by Croft, check it out here.

Oven Thermometer

Unless you have a brand new oven, it's temperature is likely inaccurate. Incorrect oven temperatures can ruin baked goods, so a oven thermometer is a must. Our favourite is the Heston Blumenthal by Salter Oven Thermometer. Check it out here.

Scales

In baking, just like in cooking meth, presition is always key. A slight mis-measure could spell a baking disaster. Our favourite is the Salter Arc Pro scale, check it out here.

Cooling Rack

Never let your cakes, cupcakes, or cookies cool in the pan set on the counter. The bottoms need cool air so they don't go soggy. Using a cooling rack is the best way to make sure your baked goods cool evenly. Our favourite is by John Lewis, check them out here.
